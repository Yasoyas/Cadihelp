Gong in Car

Summary

 This section describes the parameters in the parameter window Gong in Car of the parameter group Gong in subsystem Lift Control.

Amplitude in Car

Defines the volume for these acoustic signals inside the car (COP):

1. Arrival gong car (GA2)
2. Floor passing chime
3. In car buzzer (SUMC) for Fire (e.g. door closing) and Final Timer / Nudging

For each notification the volume can be set separately.
Available volumes:
0 = Not Used
1 = Quiet
2 = Normal
3 = Loud
4 = Very Loud

Used for arrival gong interface boards LONDAG/LONIOS

 














Delay Gong on Car


Delay after the door starts to open until the arrival gong in the car 'GAC' sounds (gong mounted on or inside the car).

If this delay is too short the gong is not audible. If it is too long the gong is activated too late to be useful. The ideal value, which depends on the door speed, is as short as possible to allow the gong to be still audible. A typical average value for most installations would be 10 (1 s).
The value 255 has a special meaning. It means that the gong arrival car is disabled. This value is the default because most installations do not use the gong in car for signalization of car arrival.





















Floors Gong on Car OFF


List of floors (max 3 floors) where the arrival gong signal in the car GAC (gong mounted on or inside the car) must not be activated (because normal gong is used instead).

A value of 0 means no floor.





















Arrival Gong COP


Gong mode for arrival gong in car with sound device on COP (SUMC) for conventional controls.
Defines when the arrival gong in car for exiting sounds (floor call served).

0: Arrival gong on COP off
1: Arrival gong on COP sounds after door starts to open and the time defined with parameter P_STR_GONG_CAR_DELAY has elapsed (standard).
2: Arrival gong on COP sounds when car starts to decelerate to floor.






















Floor Passing Gong COP


Floor passing chime in car with buzzer on COP ('SUMC').

0: OFF
1: ON (buzzer sounds when car reaches/passes position between two adjacent floor levels)
