Emergency Control

Summary

 This section describes the parameters in the parameter window Emergency Control of the parameter group Services in subsystem Lift Control.

Recall Mode

Defines the recall-mode of the elevator when (before) serving an emergency call (for emergency services NF1/2).

0: The lift first serves the registered car calls before serving the emergency call.
1: All car calls are cancelled immediately when the emergency call is assigned.
2: The lift serves all registered car call and the emergency call is only served when the car is empty (car load below minimal load limit or KL-M off)

If GRP_NF_MODE = IMMEDIATE set to 1
If GRP_NF_MODE = FREE CAR set to 2

 














Timeout


Activation time limit for emergency control (service NF1/NF2). After activation of emergency control the emergency control is switched OFF after this time if the elevator can not serve the emergency call (e.g. car blocked on other floor due to open door). 

Default value: 30s

The timeout is not effective:
- when the car is travelling
- when the control has activated DPF service after the elevator has served the emergency call

Please refer to the parameter 'DPF timeout OFF' to set the time after which the DPF service is deactivated if no car call is entered after serving the emergency call and automatic activation of the DPF service.





















Selected Lift


For emergency trip service 'NF'. Defines which lift has to answer a NF call. A value of zero means that the best available lift has to serve the NF call.

DPF Timeout Off


Activation time limit of priority trip service DPF. After activation of priority service the priority service is switched OFF again when this time elapses if no car call is entered (with the car at rest) for the priority trip.

Default value: 30s
